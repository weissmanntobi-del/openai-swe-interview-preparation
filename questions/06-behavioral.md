# Behavioral and Hiring-Manager Judgment

Use Context → Constraint → Decision → Action → Result → Reflection.

## 46. Ambiguous Ownership

Describe a project where no one clearly owned the outcome.

Strong evidence: stakeholder mapping, decision ownership, sequencing, and measurable progress.

## 47. Failed Technical Decision

Describe a design choice that proved wrong.

Strong evidence: warning signs, accountability, correction, impact control, and durable learning.

## 48. Speed Versus Correctness

Describe a time you shipped a narrower solution quickly.

Strong evidence: protected invariants, explicit limitations, staged delivery, and measurable value.

## 49. Constructive Disagreement

Describe a disagreement with a respected engineer or stakeholder.

Strong evidence: understanding the opposing view, decision criteria, experiments or data, and commitment after resolution.

## 50. Difficult Production Incident

Describe an incident where your initial hypothesis was wrong.

Strong evidence: disciplined investigation, transparent communication, safe mitigation, recovery verification, and prevention.
